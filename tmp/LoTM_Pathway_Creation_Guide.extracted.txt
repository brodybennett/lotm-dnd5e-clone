LoTM Pathway Creation Guide
A design manual for building pathways and sequence abilities with a consistent power budgetfor both human authors and AI-assisted drafting
Built for a dnd5e-derived Foundry implementation adapted to Lord of the Mysteries-style progression
How to use this guideUse it as your private framework when writing pathways by hand, and as a standing rules document when asking AI to draft sequence packages.


Purpose
This guide gives you a repeatable way to build LoTM pathways without forcing every pathway to play the same way. The core principle is simple: each sequence has a power budget, but different pathways spend that budget differently. A Seer can be balanced against a Warrior without having the same damage profile, because the Seer may spend more of its budget on information, setup, subtle control, and narrative leverage.
Use the budget as design currency, not as raw damage. A sequence is balanced when its total influence on the campaign matches its tier.
Core principles
Do not equate power with damage alone.
Price abilities by practical impact in play.
Every sequence should add a clear fantasy and a clear upgrade delta.
Upper sequences should unlock new kinds of influence, not only larger numbers.
Abstract pathways are valid as long as their leverage is bounded and priced.

Pathway construction framework
Build each sequence with the same seven-step workflow.
Write a one-sentence sequence fantasy. What does this sequence let the character do that the previous one could not?
Set the target budget. Use the total budget for a full package and the gain budget for a promotion package.
Choose a power vector. Decide how much of the budget goes into conflict, information, movement, ritual leverage, survivability, control, and narrative authority.
Draft 3-6 features: usually one signature feature, one support feature, one passive trait, and optionally one ritual or prep-based feature.
Score every feature with the rubric. Record base cost, modifiers, discounts, and final cost.
Check domain coverage so the sequence is strong where it should be strong without solving every problem at once.
Run the balance checklist. If it overruns the game, reduce reliability, frequency, scope, or setup ease before cutting the fantasy.

AI operating specification
Use this section as a standing instruction block whenever you ask AI to help build pathway content. The AI should not simply brainstorm cool ideas. It should produce a structured draft that obeys the budget and explains its scoring.
Required AI rules
Treat the sequence budget as a cap, not a suggestion.
Preserve pathway identity rather than distributing budget evenly across all domains.
Price features by actual impact in play, not by flavor text.
Bound narrative authority by scope, frequency, preparation, and reliability.
If a package exceeds budget, reduce reliability, frequency, scope, or ease of use before cutting the core fantasy.
Output both the total cost and the reasoning used to arrive at it.

Worked micro-example
Two Sequence 7 gain packages can spend the same budget very differently and still be balanced.
Warrior-like package
Enhanced melee pressure and a brief burst of physical dominance.
Danger-sense passive that raises survivability but does not reveal broad hidden truths.
Most of the spend goes into direct offense and survivability.
Approximate spend: 18.
Seer-like package
Spirit vision, omen reading, subtle misdirection, and danger intuition.
Much lower direct damage but stronger investigation, setup, and prep value.
Most of the spend goes into information, utility, and light control.
Approximate spend: 18.
Validation checklist
Does the sequence have a sharp identity rather than a pile of unrelated tricks?
Does the total or gain package stay within budget?
If the pathway is abstract, are its high-impact features bounded by frequency, scope, setup, or risk?
Would this sequence still matter if the campaign de-emphasized its best domain?
Does the package create interesting decisions rather than automatic wins?
Could another pathway of the same sequence oppose it meaningfully in at least some situations?
Does the sequence add something qualitatively new instead of only inflating previous numbers?
At high tiers, does the package change scale without erasing all need for preparation, cost, or counterplay?
